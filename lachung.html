History

LACHUNG, A SCENIC MOUNTAIN VILLAGE IN NORTH SIKKIM, WAS ONCE A VITAL TRADE ROUTE BETWEEN SIKKIM AND TIBET. DUE TO ITS STRATEGIC LOCATION NEAR THE INDO-CHINA BORDER, IT REMAINED CLOSED TO FOREIGNERS UNTIL 1999. THE VILLAGE IS RENOWNED FOR THE LACHUNG MONASTERY, BUILT IN 1880, AND IS DEEPLY ROOTED IN BUDDHIST TRADITIONS. TODAY, LACHUNG IS CELEBRATED FOR ITS BREATHTAKING LANDSCAPES, RIVER VALLEYS, WATERFALLS, AND BLOOMING RHODODENDRON FORESTS.
