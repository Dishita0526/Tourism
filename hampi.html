History

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India, was once the glorious capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, founded in 1336 CE by Harihara and Bukka Raya. Mythologically linked to Kishkindha from the Ramayana, Hampi grew into a thriving spiritual, commercial, and artistic center, especially under the reign of Krishnadevaraya in the 16th century. Known for its magnificent temples, intricate stone architecture, and vibrant bazaars, it attracted travelers from across the world. However, in 1565, after the Battle of Talikota, it was invaded and destroyed by the Deccan Sultanates, leading to its tragic decline. Rediscovered by British historians in the 19th century, Hampi today stands as a vast open-air museum of ruins, mythology, and unmatched historical grandeur.
